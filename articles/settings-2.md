**Settings** in Comparative Agility provide users with a centralized location to manage various aspects of their accounts, customize assessments, and regulate subscription details. This article offers a concise overview of the different components found within the Settings menu.

**Account Information**

The Account Information section allows users to view and modify their personal details associated with their Comparative Agility account. This includes essential information, email address, and password. Users can update their profile information to ensure accurate account details.

To learn more about resetting your password, please visit our Help Center article [**How do I change or reset password?**](https://help.comparativeagility.com/administration/how-do-i-change-my-password/)

The App Management section manages access permissions at the instance level for all Comparative Agility Apps, as well as enabling some default options. Discover how to effectively use the world’s largest agile assessment tool and a dedicated module designed to support your growth.

To learn more about what you can do here, please visit our Help Center article [**App Management**](https://help.comparativeagility.com/3-general/comparative-agility-apps/).

**Capabilities**

Capability Management empowers users to tailor their assessment process by enabling or disabling specific capabilities. By accessing the Capability Management section, users can modify capability settings, select or deselect associated statements, and fine-tune assessments to align with their organizational needs.

To learn more about capabilities, please visit our Help Center article [**Managing Capabilities**](https://help.comparativeagility.com/administration/manage-capability/).

**Data Categorization**

The Categories allow survey creators to define categories like location, preference, or role when setting up a survey. Upon completion of the survey, the feature allows for the aggregation of data not just as a whole but segmented according to these categories. This means that survey creators can filter the results based on these categories to analyze how different groups of respondents answered the survey.

To learn more about data categories, please visit our Help Center article [**Creating and Managing Data Categories**](https://help.comparativeagility.com/2-advance-topics/how-to-create-and-manage-data-categories/).

_The screenshot below shows you where to find data categorization_

**Instances**

Instances are a way for large enterprises to support an appropriate separation of data access between different groups or subsidiaries while at the same time providing a global, enterprise perspective. This is very useful, especially for large enterprises, but also for smaller organizations to better manage the focus and data access for their teams.

To learn more about creating and managing instances, please visit our Help Center article [**How to create and manage instances?**](https://help.comparativeagility.com/3-general/how-to-create-and-manage-instances/)
