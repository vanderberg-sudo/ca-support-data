# Impact Matrix — Understanding Your Team Performance

The Impact Matrix helps change leaders quickly see how teams are performing across two dimensions: **Performance** and **Cohesion**. Use it to decide where to focus your coaching energy.

## What is the Impact Matrix?

The Impact Matrix plots every team into one of four quadrants based on:

- **Performance** — the aggregate average score of the team across all survey statements
- **Cohesion** — the level of agreement between team members (not a straight standard deviation, but a proprietary measure of internal consensus)

## The Four Quadrants

**Amplify — High Performance, High Cohesion** (Green zone)
Teams here are performing well and agree with each other. Celebrate and learn from them.

**Align — High Performance, Low Cohesion** (Yellow zone)
Good results but significant disagreements internally. Worth a conversation to understand why.

**Improve — Low Performance, High Cohesion** (Yellow zone)
Teams know they have challenges and tend to take steps to fix them. Good candidates for coaching.

**Mitigate — Low Performance, Low Cohesion** (Red zone)
These teams need the most attention — they are struggling and disconnected. Prioritize these.

## How to navigate to the Impact Matrix

**From a Report:**
- Open the desired report
- Click **Impact Matrix** on the right side of the screen

**From the Overview Screen:**
- Open the Overview Screen
- Scroll down to the Impact Matrix section

## Tips for using the Impact Matrix

- Focus your time on **Mitigate** teams first — they need the most help
- Use **Amplify** teams as examples for the rest of the organization
- Don't ignore **Align** teams — high disagreement can hide underlying issues even when scores look good
- Run the survey every 3–6 months to track movement between quadrants over time
