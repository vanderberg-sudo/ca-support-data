This article will guide you through the steps of using our new editor. The Editor will give you a fully customized experience for all your needs in terms of creating a survey.

### Contents

  * **Dimensions**
  * **Statements**
  * **General Settings**



### **Dimensions**

Once you open the **Editor** , a default dimension will appear. By selecting the **Dimension field** , you can type in the name of your dimension.

If you would like to add a new dimension, whether it is with or without characteristics, simply:

  * Navigate to the **sidebar** on the left-hand side
  * Click on **New Dimension**
  * Proceed with **naming it** and **adding statements**



_In the short video below we show you how to add and rename your dimension_

**Note** : You can always hide the Dimensions section by clicking on the arrow.

Comparative Agility gives you the ability to add dimensions from our genuine surveys:

  * Open **Genuine Surveys** in the upper right corner
  * Locate the **desired survey**
  * Expand it to find the **dimension** that suits you best
  * **Drag & drop** it to its most suitable place



_The short video below shows you how to add dimensions from genuine surveys_

### **Statements**

Clicking on the **\+ icon** gives you the option to create a **new statement**. After entering your statement, you will be presented with the same set of options available for all other statements, such as **question type** , **tooltips, anchors** , **recommendations** , or **conditions**. We will cover each of these later in the article.

_In the short video below we show you how to add a new statement_

**Question type**

You can choose from several question types, including **Fist of Five** , **Likert** , **Multiple Choice** , **Open-Ended** , **Ranking** , **Satisfaction Index** , **Scales** , and **Word Cloud**. Based on your survey's goals, you can use one question type throughout or mix and match different types across your survey.

To change the question type, simply click the **dropdown menu** and select your preferred option.

To add answers, click the field and enter your responses. For certain question types, you can also **rearrange the answers** by moving them **up** or **down** to get the order just right.

_The short video below shows you how to create questions and answers_

**Tooltips**

**Tooltips** can be utilized to provide **additional information** about statements to survey respondents, thereby enabling them to answer questions more accurately. To add a tooltip, simply select the statement and type in the necessary information on the right-hand side.

_In the short video below we show you how to add tooltips_

**Note** : You can easily **upload an image** or **delete** the tooltip altogether.

**Anchors**

With the Anchors option, you will be able to **modify the statement 's possible responses**. You can select from predefined responses or add your own custom response. Additionally, this option allows you to enable or disable the '**Not Applicable** ' choice, which is commonly used in our standard surveys.

_In the short video below we show you how to use anchors_

**Reversed scoring** reverses the scores assigned to each response option so that the highest score corresponds to the most negative response and the lowest score corresponds to the most positive response.

**Copy and Paste statements**

If you would like to copy and paste an already existing statement, simply **select the statement** , click on the **Copy Question** option, select the statement under which you would like to paste the copied one, and proceed with the **Paste Question** option.

_The short video below shows you how to copy and paste statements_

**Recommendations**

This option will allow you to **see current** and **create new recommendations**. Select the **statement** and open the **Recommendations tab** to use this option.

_In the screenshot below we show you how to use the recommendation option_

**Conditions**

**Conditions** are essential annotations that surface when users select specific answers. These comments serve to offer further context into the rationale behind the user's selection of a particular response. Conditional comments can be applied across various statements as deemed appropriate by the author.

To add conditional comments, select the **statement** , open the **Conditions tab,** and click on the **Add condition** option. Select which **anchor** will trigger a **Comment Box**.

_The short video below shows you how to add conditions_

**Link to World Index**

By default, each statement in our genuine surveys is linked to the **World Index;** this option allows you to remove that connection.

### General Settings

Comparative Agility offers a variety of options that you could utilize in your surveys.

_In the screenshot below we show you where to find the general settings_

**Comments**

  * **Enable End of Survey comment - **a comment section will be added at the end of the survey, giving the respondents a place to share their thoughts
  * **Enable Optional Comments - **will give the respondents the possibility to add a comment tied to a specific statement
  * **Randomize anchors - **will give the respondents the same anchors but in a different order



_The screenshot below shows you where to find the comments section_

**Recommendations**

With this section, you have the ability to enable/disable recommendations that will be shown later on the report:

  * **Author –** Created and edited by experts and authors within Comparative Agility, author recommendations offer specialized insights tailored to specific assessments.
  * **Custom –** Users have the ability to create their own recommendations, allowing for a personalized approach to improving agility within their organization.
  * **CA-Curated –** These recommendations are generated by AI and curated by Comparative Agility experts. They provide valuable insights based on data analysis and industry best practices.



_In the screenshot below we show you where to find the recommendations section_

**Anchors direction**

Comparative Agility gives you the ability to choose between **descending** , **random** , or **ascending** anchor direction.

_The screenshot below shows you where to find the anchors direction section_

**Anchors**

You have the flexibility to select from a range of Anchors, including **Frequency** , **Level of Agreement** , and others, or create **custom anchors**.
