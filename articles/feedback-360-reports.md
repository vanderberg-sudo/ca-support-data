# Feedback 360 &#8211; Reports

**URL:** https://help.comparativeagility.com/feedback-360/feedback-360-reports/
**Category:** Feedback 360

---

This article will walk you through the 360-degree feedback report. Feedback 360 is a process that gathers input from managers, peers, and direct reports to provide a well-rounded view of an employee’s performance and development needs.

**Navigating to the Report**

  * Open the **survey tab**
  * Navigate to the **View Report** button



_In the short video below we show you how to open a report_

**Overview**

The overview provides dimensions and averages for each role that took the survey. Open the **dimensions** for further details.

_The screenshot below shows you the overview_

**Insights tab**

The insights tab gives you the information about your **Key Strengths** and **Key Opportunities**. Dive deeper into the results and see how people responded.

**Area of Strengths** summarizes the competency areas where you received the highest feedback scores. It reflects how your strengths are perceived across different roles and relationships in your organization.

**Hidden Strengths** shows areas where others rated you highly, but you rated yourself lower. These may be strengths you underestimate—insights that can boost your self-awareness and confidence.

_In the screenshot below we show you the key strengths tab_

**Areas of Improvement** highlight the competency areas where feedback suggests room for growth. Use it to identify patterns and opportunities to enhance your impact across roles and relationships.

**Blind Spots** highlights areas where you rated yourself higher than others did. These gaps in perception can point to behaviors or skills that may need attention or clearer communication.

_The screenshot below shows you the key opportunities tab_

**Statements**

Under the statements tab, we list all the statements from the survey with their averages, based on the results. You will be able to see **Key Strengths** and **Key Opportunities** highlighted. By expanding each statement, you will be able to see how each role responded.

_In the video below we show you the statements tab_

**Notes**

**Reflections** are related to a particular report and are usually used to identify opportunities for improvement.

**Additional insights**

This tab will show all the **comments** people left while taking the survey.

**Options**

Last but not least, you have the ability to **export** the report as **CSV** and **Excel** , **print it** , save it as a **PDF** , and **share the report** with others.
